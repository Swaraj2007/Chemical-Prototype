Process

Follow these steps to prepare liquid soap. This is a simplified lab-scale process.

Castor Oil+KOH‚ÜíPotassium Soap+Glycerol
  1. Measure and pour 20ml of distilled water into a beaker.
  2. Slowly add 5g of KOH and stir until completely disolved.
  3. Add 10ml of castor oil gratually while stirring.
  4. Add 10ml of Methanol to enhance the mixing.
  5. Heat mixture gently and stir continuosly.
  6. Add 5g of Glycerol and 5g of Boric acid.
  7. Continue stirring until a homogeneous liquid is forms.
  8. Cool and store in a lebelled bottle

Advantages

  • Easy to make at home with simple ingredients.
  • Customizable scents and properties (e.g., moisturizing).
  • Eco-friendly if using natural bases.
  • Cost-effective compared to store-bought soap.
  • Educational for learning basic chemistry and safety.

Disadvantages

  • Requires precise measurements to avoid separation or spoilage.
  • May not be as stable as commercial products without preservatives.
  • Potential for skin irritation if not made correctly.
  • Time-consuming process (heating, cooling, setting).
  • Limited shelf life without proper storage.
